<\/h3>\n

I write poetry for the hopeful and big hearted.<\/h3>\n

My big dream is for\u00a0us all to become a little more human.<\/p>\n

See…<\/p>\n

…we’ve built\u00a0a world that’s killed\u00a0most of what it means to be a beautiful human. The acceptance, the love, the compassion, the hope, the courage, the empathy.<\/p>\n

And a world without these fragments is a shallow, cold, and fearful place.<\/p>\n

In the place of these brilliant and shining humans lay\u00a0creatures made of stone and apathy. Boredom and decay.<\/p>\n

I\u00a0speak for\u00a0the subtle aspects of our hearts and souls we’ve forgotten, yet yearn for.<\/p>\n

I speak for the great mother we all share, and how to return to the sacred.<\/p>\n

I speak for the lost and confused, and those who exist in pieces, because you are full in your brokenness.<\/p>\n

If this resonates, then I ask you to join me<\/a>.<\/p>\n

My creative lineage is steeped in the ancient philosophers, poets, and sages of times past. I write because I can’t not write.<\/p>\n

I write because I don’t know what else to do. When I don’t write a small piece of me starts to die\u00a0– I\u00a0become malnourished, and it’s not a pretty sight.<\/p>\n

When I do write, I start to love life again. The tap to my universal happiness faucet begins to flow. The world is usually a better place when I write, at least my inner world.<\/p>\n

And this makes me a more useful poet to you.<\/p>\n
