Is home where we originally laid roots as a child?\u00a0<\/em><\/p>\n

Or, is it where we lie our heads and bodies for the night?<\/em><\/p>\n

Home isn’t a place, it’s a state of mind and way of living that can make the world your home. We’ve outgrown the American Dream – the promise of a house, a yard, a wife, a regular child and a half person (look up the stats).<\/p>\n

Home no longer means place. It’s an ever-evolving definition, and we run into problems when we start to chain this definition to a material thing or an idea.<\/p>\n

Home has never been a place.<\/p>\n

It’s been about the people, the pain, the laughs, the deep-rooted comfort. A place to sink your roots in and build something. A life, a project, a work of art, a relationship.<\/p>\n

In an age where tiny-homes matter, where cities are being re-built, and the suburban dream continues to fade away into nothing more than an ember we must change how we view our homes.<\/p>\n

A couch can be a home. The bed shared with a stranger can be your home.<\/p>\n

A tree in the forest can be your home.<\/p>\n

Finding home and love within the cracks of yourself will allow the definition of home to expand to wherever you are.<\/p>\n

Someday the time might come to lay deep roots.<\/p>\n

Until that time comes\u00a0\u2014\u00a0ramble on.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
