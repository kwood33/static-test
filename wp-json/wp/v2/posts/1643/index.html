At some point the adult version of ourselves decides the world isn\u2019t fun or magical anymore, we decide it\u2019s time to get to work and grow up.<\/p>\n

This is a critical mistake.<\/p>\n

The world is always magic. The world is always alive.<\/p>\n

A child’s eyes see reality’s potential. It\u2019s not the naivet\u00e9 it\u2019s commonly marked out to be. It\u2019s connected more closely to truth than our rigid eyes can ever hope to see.<\/p>\n

We can cultivate this pure vision once again.<\/p>\n

We can return to truth,\u00a0joy, and freedom.<\/p>\n

We can return to a wandering existence.<\/p>\n

When was the last time you asked a child, \u201cWhat\u2019s your purpose in life?\u201d. Most likely, never.<\/p>\n

Their purpose is to exist, laugh, play, and imagine.<\/p>\n

Our need to wear grown up glasses has caused us to lose\u00a0our beautiful lens of imagination. Instead we contribute to a failing system that forces our unique spheres of potential into tiny square boxes.<\/p>\n

We lose the magic when we try to control.<\/p>\n

Magic and imagination go hand-in-hand. They are free flowing. Colorful, bright and constantly changing.<\/p>\n

Imagine more and think less.<\/i><\/p>\n

We\u2019ve forgotten how to imagine. Ask an adult to use their imagination and most likely they\u2019ll craft a few different approaches to how a situation will turn out.<\/p>\n

Options, A, B, C.<\/p>\n

What about the impossible ones? The ones that only a naive child can attain. They are nowhere to be found.<\/p>\n

We become so wrapped up in our current framework of the world that it constrains what\u2019s actually possible.<\/p>\n

We end up choking on our own definitions.<\/p>\n

\n

The intuitive mind is a sacred gift and the rational mind is a faithful servant.\u00a0We have created a society that honors the servant and has forgotten the gift.\u201d <\/i><\/p>\n

\u2014 Albert Einstein<\/i><\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

When we choose solely from our current possibilities we leave a lot on the table, we use our minds to draw from past experiences that\u2019ve been tainted by our perceptions. We\u2019re already operating from lack.<\/p>\n

Fixing this is simple.<\/p>\n

Become more childish.<\/p>\n

Do what makes you come alive.<\/p>\n

Laugh more.<\/p>\n

Stop taking yourself so seriously.<\/p>\n

Sing out loud.<\/p>\n

Dance like an idiot.<\/p>\n

Pretend you\u2019re a secret agent, protecting your hotel from bad-guys.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s time to bring imagination back.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
