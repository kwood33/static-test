is home the dividing line
\nand fence that separates us from others
\nis it the walls that held our tears and our joy,
\nour traditions and pictures
\nis that where home lies?<\/p>\n

is home the stories you\u2019re afraid to tell
\nwhere you could shut the door and live in your imagination
\nwhere you knew what it meant to make people proud,
\nor to let them down,
\nis home expectation?<\/p>\n

is home in the mountain streams,
\nin the trees who were the only ones who
\ncould truly hear our problems and dreams.
\nis home what we loved?<\/p>\n

because now I don\u2019t know what home is<\/p>\n

and I can\u2019t look for it,
\nin old definitions of what home was,
\nbecause maybe it never was those things,
\nand maybe instead:
\nit was a feeling,
\nhowever small and however subtle,
\nhowever rare it occurred,
\nmaybe that was home,
\nand it was different for us all.<\/p>\n

I keep looking to find home,
\nin the past.
\nin old memories,
\nthat are now sepia toned,
\nin these past glimpses of happiness
\nand sadness,
\nand learning,
\njoy and mistakes.
\nI look for home here.
\nI look for home in the old gifts,
\nand boxes of photos that show me I used to be happy,
\nand I used to be content,
\nI look for home here
\nand don\u2019t find it.<\/p>\n

I find perhaps some flavor of grief,
\nfor everything that\u2019s changed.
\nand it hurts to remember sometimes,
\nbut maybe that hurting means it actually mattered to us,
\nand we must force ourselves to bear the pain of lost homes and lost loves<\/p>\n

the uncertainty of this journey of defining what home is,
\nor even experiencing it again,
\nbecause you get used to being uprooted,
\nand you get used to never feeling comfortable
\nto never feeling home,<\/p>\n

even if you have a home.
\neven if you have a family,
\neven if you have nothing.<\/p>\n

maybe home is nothing but a fleeting feeling,
\nand maybe it is in the past,
\nand maybe it is in the future,
\nand maybe it\u2019s right now.
\ninside you,
\nit\u2019s in a book that makes you gasp,
\nit\u2019s in a photo of your first love,
\nit\u2019s in a ring your dad gave you before he passed,
\nand maybe it\u2019s in a song that transports you back to a flavorful moment you wish to get lost in again.<\/p>\n

maybe it\u2019s about taking all those things,
\nand carrying them with us,
\nso we can remember what home was
\nand what things actually mattered,
\nso when we stumble upon that moment again we won\u2019t miss it,
